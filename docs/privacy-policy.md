# Certora Privacy Policy

**Status:** Product draft for legal review  
**Effective date:** 19 July 2026  
**Controller:** Certora (replace with the registered legal entity and address before launch)

## 1. Scope

This policy explains how Certora processes information when recruiters create, issue, score, review, or administer assessments and when candidates complete an issued assessment.

## 2. Information we collect

- Account and workspace data, such as name, work email, role, organization, and authentication records.
- Assessment data, including questions, answers, uploaded work, spreadsheets, formulas, code, scores, reviewer notes, and submission timestamps.
- Assessment security data, including fullscreen changes, tab visibility, focus changes, restricted shortcut attempts, clipboard attempts, browser/device checks, warning counts, and security event timestamps.
- Proctoring data only when the assessment is configured for it and the candidate gives the required consent, such as camera or microphone permission, proctoring evidence, model-derived signals, and review outcomes.
- Technical data needed to operate the service, such as IP address, browser metadata, error logs, and session identifiers.

## 3. How we use information

We use information to authenticate users, deliver assessments, save candidate work, calculate scores, detect assessment integrity signals, support recruiter review, prevent abuse, provide support, maintain security, improve the service, and comply with legal obligations.

The proctoring model provides signals for review. It does not make an irreversible employment decision by itself. Recruiters are responsible for appropriate human review and fair use.

## 4. Sharing and processors

We share data only with service providers needed to operate the product, such as hosting, database, email, object storage, video delivery, monitoring, and model infrastructure providers. Providers must be configured with appropriate access controls and contractual protections. We do not sell candidate assessment data.

## 5. Retention

The default retention schedule is defined in `docs/data-retention-and-deletion.md`. The recruiter organization may configure a shorter period where the product supports it. Legal holds, fraud investigations, security incidents, and unresolved disputes may require limited retention for longer.

## 6. Candidate rights

Subject to applicable law, candidates may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or information about automated processing. Requests should be sent to the organization that issued the assessment or to the Certora privacy contact configured for production.

## 7. Security

Certora uses authenticated access, short-lived issued assessment tokens, role checks, audit logs, encrypted transport in production, restricted storage access, and bounded proctoring event logs. No browser-only control can guarantee prevention of screenshots, operating-system task switching, or device tampering.

## 8. Changes and contact

The registered legal entity, privacy contact, supervisory authority information, international transfer language, and jurisdiction-specific notices must be completed before production launch. Material changes should be communicated through the product or the issuing organization.

This document is a product implementation draft, not legal advice.
